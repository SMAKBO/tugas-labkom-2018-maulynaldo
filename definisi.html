Ethylene Oxide Definition

Ethylene oxide, also known as oxyane is an organic compound with the formula C2H4O molecule. This compound is cyclic ethers. Ethylene oxide colorless flammable gas form at outside temperature and sweet smelling. This compound is the simplest epoxide: a three-member ring with 1 oxygen and 2 carbon. Due to its molecular structure, ethylene oxide is widely used in addition reactions, such as polymerization. Ethylene oxide dihydrogen with acetaldehyde and vinyl Alcohol.
