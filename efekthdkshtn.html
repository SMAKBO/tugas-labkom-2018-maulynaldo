Effects on Health

Inhaled

Short-term Exposure : Excessive exposure can cause irritation of the eyes, nose, throat and respiratory tract, effects on central nervous system, dizziness, convulsions and coma, blood effects, pulmonary damage, delayed pulmonary edema, cancer.

First Aid Measures : If it is safe to enter the area, keep it away from exposure. Use a mask or similar device to perform artificial respiration (safety respiration) if necessary. Maintain body temperature and rest. Immediately take it to the doctor.

Swallowed

Short-Term Exposure : Toxic. Exposure is associated with carcinogenic and reproductive effects, spontaneous abortion, nerve poisoning and decreased fertility rates in males and fetal death, as well as mutagenic changes.

First Aid Measures : Do not be stimulated to vomit. If the victim is conscious give milk or plenty of water. Do not be stimulated to vomit or give drink to an unconscious victim. If all of a sudden the ingredients entering into the mouth in small amounts can be rinsed with water until the taste of the material is lost. Immediately take it to the doctor.

Skin Contact

Short-Term Exposure: Can be absorbed through the skin. Causes an allergic response. May cause blisters and burns when touching. Effects can be delayed.

Eye Contact

Short-Term Exposure: Irritation of the cornea, injured when in contact with the ingredients. Possible causes of cataracts.
